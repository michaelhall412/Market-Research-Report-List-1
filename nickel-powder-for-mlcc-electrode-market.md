<p><p>Nickel powder for MLCC (Multilayer Ceramic Capacitor) electrodes is produced using two primary methods: CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition) and PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition). The CVD method involves depositing nickel onto a substrate through chemical reactions, resulting in high-purity, uniform powder suited for electrode applications. In contrast, the PVD method utilizes physical processes, such as sputtering or evaporation, to create nickel layers. Both methods cater to the growing demand for enhanced performance in electronic components and offer distinct advantages in quality and production efficiency.</p></p>
