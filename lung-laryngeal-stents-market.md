<p><p>The lung laryngeal stents market comprises three primary types: self-expandable stents, balloon-expandable stents, and non-expandable stents. Self-expandable stents automatically enlarge upon placement, providing consistent support to the airway. Balloon-expandable stents require inflation to achieve their final diameter, offering precise deployment and adjustable sizing. Non-expandable stents are designed for temporary support without permanent expansion, serving specific clinical needs. Each type fulfills distinct therapeutic requirements, catering to patients with varying degrees of airway obstruction or laryngeal issues.</p></p>
