<p><ul><li>SBS</li><li>SIS</li><li>SEBS</li><li>SEPS</li><li>Compound Type</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Styrene-based thermoplastic elastomers (TPEs) are categorized into several types based on their chemical structure and properties. SBS (Styrene-Butadiene-Styrene) offers good elasticity and resilience, making it versatile for various applications. SIS (Styrene-Isoprene-Styrene) features enhanced elasticity and is often used in adhesives. SEBS (Styrene-Ethylene-Butylene-Styrene) provides superior thermal stability and UV resistance, suitable for outdoor use. SEPS (Styrene-Ethylene-Propylene-Styrene) combines flexibility with toughness. Compound types involve blends with fillers for specific performance traits, while "Other" categories include innovative formulations tailored for niche applications.</p></p>
